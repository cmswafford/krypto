How To Play

Rules

Each game has one target number and five low numbers. The target number ranges between 30 and 50. The low numbers are between 1 and 20. Numbers cannot be used more than once.

The objective of the game is to create a mathematical equation using each of the five low numbers exactly once. The result of this equation must have the same result as the target number.

You may use the basic algebraic operators (+, -, *, /) as well as exponents. Use the caret notation (^) to indicate an exponent.

Factorial function is allowed!

Taking roots is also permissable, and the numerator is given "for free". For example, if you have a 16 and 2, you can get to 24 by typing (16^(1/2))! which of course equates to 4 factorial.

There are 15,504 different hands of low numbers and 325,584 possible games.

Tips

  • If you have left over numbers try to use them to create 1 to use for multiplication.
  • Recognize target squares 36 and 49 then aim for a solution of 62 or 72 respectively.
